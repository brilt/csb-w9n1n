How do we plan to go there?

To go to Finland, we will all take the plane. Three companies are fighting for the French-Finnish air market; Finnair, Easyjet and Blue1. We will all arrive at Helsinki airport on a Finnair flight. To get to the airport we decided to take the train.

Ines and Killian will take the train in Angers-Saint-Laud on February 10th to go to Paris Charles de Gaulle 2 airport. Thomas and Emma don't know for now how they will go there but they will take the plane from Paris Beauvais airport on February 11th .

We will all arrive at Helsinki Vantaa airport, Killian and Ines on February 10th and Thomas and Emma on the 11th. We will then stay in Helsinki 1 or 2 days to visit the city and then we will go by train to Seinäjoki.

How to get around once you arrive

You can travel in Finland by various different means, wether it is by plane, bus, car, train, steamship, cruise ship, bicycle and even skis or or sleigh.

Here are a few information about these different means of transport:

Plane: There are 27 airport in Finland, 5 of them have international flights. The main one is Helsinki-Vantaa. The national finnish airline isis called Finnair.

Car: It is very tricky in winter because the roads are slippery. Actualy, snow tires are legally required between December and February). You must also be careful of reindeers and elks which frequently wander onto the roads.

Train: The railway network is rated one of the best in Europe. It spreads throughout the country (from Helsinki to Kolari).

Bike: You will be ables to find bikes to rent in most cities.

Boat: Thanks to its many lakes and the sea surrounding it, you will easily find all kinds of boat services.
