Tips about everyday life

  • Finnish tap water is some of the best in the world.
  • You need to wave for a bus to stop.
  • The Finnish public transit system is well managed and usually on time.
  • Finns eat lunch quite early compared to other countries.
  • Alcohol is sold in stores between 9am and 9pm.
  • You can only buy alcoholic beverages with more than 22% alcohol from the age of 20 and only at certain times of the day.
  • Product names are usually displayed only in Finnish and Swedish.
  • Cash is used less and less in Finland.
  • Tipping is not mandatory nor expected in Finland.
  • Home deliveries are usually not delivered to your door.
  • Finns celebrate a lot of different national and religious holidays.

Tips from former ERASMUS students

  • Take advantage of your Erasmus to visit many countries!
  • Try to find the time to go to Lapland, going to see the Northern Lights or dog sledding is an incredible experience.
  • Taking a guide to see the Northern Lights will cost you 110€ per person but is really worth it.
  • Try to go out and meet people, the finnish are distant at first but they have a good level of English and are actually very friendly once you start to know them.
  • The cold may throw you off at first but you will get used to it.
  • You can buy a student card that will give you interesting discounts and only costs 25€ for 6 months. (Very usefull as renting a car is quite coplicated and expensive.)

Culture Shock

  • Finns eat lunch quite early compared to other countries.
  • Finns speak English very well, which is very different from the French.
  • The Finnish public transit system is well managed and usually on time.
  • During the winter months, temperatures can sometimes drop to -35° Celsius (France temperatures are generally positive or almost).
  • With the "right of everyone", it is allowed to walk on private domains (natural areas like forests, mountains in the north of the country or on lakes and rivers, ...) without the permission of the local owner.
  • Finland is one of the safest countries in the world.
  • In Finland there are a lot of mosquitoes because of the lakes.
  • It snows!
  • Tipping is not mandatory nor expected in Finland.
  • Home deliveries are usually not delivered to your door.
  • The food is not good enough for our French taste buds.
  • As soon as we go out of the city all the services are very far away.
  • The cold and the polar night are sometimes difficult to live.
  • In Finland, the cows of the fields are replaced by reindeers of breeding.
  • It can be night several days or months in a row as well as day several months in a row depending on the season and your location in the country.
